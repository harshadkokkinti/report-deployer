Finding 01

Staff accused of lying to visitors about tickets

From the review
“SUMMARY: * Big disappointment compared to premier aquariums like the Shedd in Chicago or Baltimore. Sparse exhibits that are not designed well for large crowds, and a lot of digital displays rather than actual sea life exhibits. * At $65 per person, not worth the visit. That said, I am still giving it 2 stars because even the worst aquarium has beautiful things worth seeing. * The highlight was the volunteers. They are SO nice and SO helpful. * The staff (not volunteers) are trained to deceive visitors into purchasing tickets online and lie when asked how to purchase a ticket on site. (I know - bold accusation - but see the details below.) DETAILS: We visited in June 2026 on a weekday. When we arrived, there was no obvious way to purchase a ticket. There were roped-off lines but no obvious signs for tickets. We saw a poster with a large QR code on it. We realized we had to purchase tickets online. I prefer using cash because its usually easier and does not require giving up personal info. I decided to use the QR code. I was required to first create an account. (As a one-day visitor?!) I stopped and asked the young man by the roped line if there was any way to purchase a ticket on site. He said the only way was to purchase online. I said, "You're telling me the only way to buy a ticket is to go online? That's ridiculous!" He responded by saying "Yup." I went back online as a 2nd staff person arrived. I asked him the same question, and he said the only way to purchase a ticket was online. (I was very frustrated at this point.) I gave up and started buying the tickets on my phone when my friend asked the 2nd man what the line by the side of the building was for. He said it was for people who didn't have a cell phone or couldn't purchase online. SERIOUSLY?! Both of these men had just bald faced lied about having to purchase a ticket online! We got into that line to purchase tickets. (I won't bore you with the tactics they use to make that line run more slowly than necessary. Again, a bold accusation, but I can back up my claim.) The line to purchase tickets had 2 people running registers, while there were 8 people standing around waiting for people who purchased tickets online. And to make things worse, we could only use a credit card to buy tickets at the register - they did not accept cash. After finally getting in, we went to a booth staffed by volunteers. There was an older man and woman standing there. I only mention they were older because I am older too and often feel "invisible" in public. I asked to see a customer rep to explain what happened. The man was very nice and took me to find someone to help. Who does he find--the 2nd man who lied to me about buying tickets. I said I would not talk to him because he lied to me. The volunteer quickly said he would find someone else. He then brought a real customer relations person to speak with me. The CR person arrived and listened to my complaint. She essentially confirmed they train their staff to push visitors hard to purchase online, but not to lie. She said it was to keep the lines moving. (Both of her staff members lied to me-and I don't think they lied on their own-I think they were trained to respond that way.) I told her I objected to having to create an account to buy a ticket and asked why they don't offer guest checkout to minimize the collection of personal data. All she said was she would pass my comments along. (I could go on about how unsatisfying talking to her was, but I'll spare you.) We finally started our aquarium visit and it was very disappointing. The exhibits are sparse. There aren't a lot of fish or sea life to see. Even on a light visitor day, it was very difficult to see exhibits up close based on the layout, and some sections of the aquarium are mostly digital exhibits. I counted at least 3 digital exhibits to every 1 exhibit that contained sea creatures in one section. Having recently visited the Shedd Aquarium in Chicago (which is amazing BTW!) I cannot fully describe how disappointing the Monterey Bay Aquarium is.”

Finding 02

Long-time visitor reports first-ever disappointment

From the review
“I've been coming here for about 30 years now, always a childhood favorite of mine, especially as a local. I went today and was honestly surprised and disappointed for the first time ever, due to the complete removal of "The Splash Zone". I have people come from way out of town, just to see that section and the penguins and it's just gone. It's nothing but employee facilities now and totally walled off. Due to this, it was also absolutely slammed beyond usual tourism, you couldn't even comfortably maneuver around the facility because people were letting their children run a muck and staff never intervened either, even when children were lying down right in the middle of where people are meant to walk. I almost tripped over multiple unattended small children this visit, which is a major safety hazard given how many elderly and handicapped people are often there. The volunteers also were totally fine letting children harass the Rays in the petting pool, so they were so scared we didn't get to see them. This was not nearly as much of an issue in the past when there was an actual place for the kids to play and explore. We were left with significantly less to see and do on this trip and I felt a need to apologize to my friends for the unexpected changes, as I was unaware. Would happily leave a positive review again if this changes one day. To anyone else really hoping to see the penguins and other amazing splash zone creatures, don't waste your time, they're gone and I doubt they're coming back.”
