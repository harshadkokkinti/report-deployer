Finding 01

20-year loyal customer left devastated by service collapse.

From the review
“My wife and I have been coming to Max Brenner for close to 20 years. About a decade ago I left this review: "5/5 stars. It has amazing desserts and also executes extremely well with any regular dishes. Most would assume it's a dessert place only. If in a rush or don't want to deal with a wait, I recommend coming for to-go hot chocolate. During the holidays they run a booth in Bryant Park." I meant every word of it. Tonight I would give zero stars if Google allowed it. We walked in to a dead quiet room and stood at the bar for over five minutes without a single greeting. Eventually someone pointed us to a touchscreen kiosk to place our order. At a place charging $25+ for two small cups of hot chocolate, a self-service machine is not the experience you are selling. The drinks were unrecognizable. We ordered the Italian thick hot chocolate, which over the years has been closer to solid than liquid. Tonight it was watered down to the point of being flavorless. Only in the very last few sips did anything familiar come through. The staff member working the bar wasn't trained in how to properly make the drinks or engage with customers. When we raised our concerns with the manager about the quality of the drinks, it went nowhere. This place clearly struggled through Covid and the market conditions since have not been kind either. But a $25 snack with kiosk ordering, undrinkable drinks, and no hospitality is not a tough economy problem. That is a standards problem. Max Brenner, if you are still reading these reviews: either care again or close gracefully. Twenty years of loyalty deserved better than tonight.”

Finding 02

Valentine's Day disaster: 90+ minutes waited, food arrived cold.

From the review
“I've celebrated many holidays at Max Brenner in the past, birthdays, Mother's Day, Valentine's Day. It was always amazing service and food. I don't know what changed but this experience was a disaster. I noticed there were many negative reviews recently, but I've always been the type to take things with a grain of salt and be my own judge. Trust me: formulate a different opinion at a different restaurant. My boyfriend and I traveled 2.5 hrs by train for a 2:00 PM reservation. We arrived at 1:50 to an overbooked, sweaty, angry lobby. So many people were there, it wasn't clear what line was for checking in versus takeout. Despite our reservation, we weren't seated until 2:59 PM. When asked if we could be called/texted instead of standing and waiting, we were told if we left the cramped room for air, we'd lose our spot. I want to add,, all the workers in the front and our waitress were nice given the circumstances. However, by interacting with anyone, you felt their vibes were extremelyyyyy overwhelmed in the chaotic environment, which nobody coming to spend money wants to be greeted with the second they walk in. Uneasiness, stress, anxiousness everywhere. Once seated a minute shy of 1 hour past our reservation,, the waitress insisted we order everything at once so the kitchen could "space it out." I told her I was a little indecisive, because if we order now I don't want everything coming all at once- I'd want drinks appetizers then desserts. I was reassured giving our order upfront would be the best case scenario for everyone. Ok cool makes sense. WELLLLL our desserts arrived literally 5 minutes after ordering. When I told the runner we hadn't had drinks or apps yet, he rudely said, "Well this is what's ready for you now." My boyfriend and I are very non confrontational people, so we took it, despite knowing it went against what we specified. When she walked by, our waitress just laughed awkwardly at the mistake.”
